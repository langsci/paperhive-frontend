Join the PaperHive team

PaperHive is a software company in the field of scientific research and collaboration. After becoming a part of the Centre for Entrepreneurship incubator at the TU Berlin in mid 2015 and receiving the EXIST startup grant since December 2015, the company is releasing its product in Q2 2016.

PaperHive is the coworking hub for researchers, aiming to support knowledge-creators in their work and to dramatically increase open collaboration in science.

We strive for high diversity in our team and encourage you to apply regardless of your (and our) identity.

Software engineer AngularJS MEAN stack full-time

We are looking for an incredibly creative developer willing to take over responsibility for engineering our existing application in a user-friendly and sustainable way.

Your tasks

Together with our team you will

  • advance the component-based AngularJS application
  • constantly decide how the overall architecture can be improved
  • implement reliable and efficient algorithms for processing and displaying large amounts of data (e.g., large documents)
  • upgrade the codebase to Angular 2
  • improve the development toolchain
  • enhance the continuous integration and continuous delivery pipeline
  • optimize the UX and overall appearance (with a designer)
  • realize {{yourIdeas}}

Optional: if you're experienced with backend development you can also advance our REST API (Node.js, MongoDB and Elasticsearch).

Numerous other interesting tasks will arise along the way. Bringing in your own ideas and taking over responsibility is most important to us.

Your profile

We need a team player to join PaperHive in May who
  • has a bachelor's or master's degree in computer science (or a related field)
  • has ≥1.5 years of development experience with AngularJS
  • wants to stay at the forefront of web technology by learning and applying new concepts every day
  • loves to build reusable code with accompanying unit tests
  • makes decisions based on facts instead of buzzwords
  • speaks English or German fluently

Experience with large-scale web applications, backend development (Node.js, MongoDB, Elasticsearch), TypeScript, ES6 or Angular 2 is a plus.
